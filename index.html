What Stands in the Way Becomes the Way

Software Development Philosophy

To the layperson, computer programming seems almost like magic. Every year, our ability to manipulate the world through programs and applications grows. From audio recognition software giving you the name of a song on the radio, to self-driving cars, to targeted advertising, to at-home medical testing. A person can, without touching anything and while standing alone in a room, speak words and 30 minutes later a pizza will arrive at their door, all without them interacting with another human being. Those of us with experience in software development know better.

We know that it IS magic.

A small business that, 20 years ago, would have reached perhaps 100 paying customers a month can now reach 10,000 a day. A charity that could only raise hundreds of dollars a month and help only a half-dozen people can now do 10 times, 20 times, 100 times as much. Through incredibly complex tracking and logistics systems, every day millions of packages move from state to state, and billions from country to country.

The power that computer programming conveys demands a level of responsibility. To build a website or application for a person, business, or organization is to implicitly endorse them. Furthermore, you are explicitly empowering them and their mission by doing so. If computer programming is magic, it is a dangerous kind of magic that must be used with wisdom and caution.

To this end, I use the best of my judgement to discern clients that I am willing to work for. Clients that would manipulate or defraud, that would oppress or discriminate, or that would profit from fear or ignorance will not be clients of mine. The right path is, unfortunately, often not the easy path. While I may ask several questions about how your business or organization operates that might not seem relevant to your ideas about a website, know that they are asked out of a desire to do the right thing.

If you desire to do the right thing as well, if your organization has nothing to fear from the truth, if you wish to make the world a better place than it was, then I would be honored to share some of that magic with you.
